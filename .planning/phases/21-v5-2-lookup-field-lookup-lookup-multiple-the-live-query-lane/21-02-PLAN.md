<objective>
Mirror the lookup wire surface into the .NET backend byte-identically, and teach the .NET tree-validator about `SearchAction`.

Purpose: the two backends are kept byte-aligned by the parity suite, and gotcha #8 is the specific way this silently breaks — a new nullable without `WhenWritingNull` emits `"field": null`, and a new optional bool without `WhenWritingDefault` emits `"allowCustom": false`, either of which drifts from the TS twin (which omits both) and fails strict consumers. Both tree-walkers must change in the same phase or a duplicate `searchAction` name is a hard failure on one backend and a silent pass on the other.

Output: a `LookupItem` record; `Selected`, `Candidates`, `SearchBind`, `SearchAction`, `AllowCustom` APPENDED to the `FieldNode` positional record with correct ignore conditions; a `SearchAction` line in `Collect`; a `LookupSerializationTests.cs` proving absent-vs-present for each.
</objective>
